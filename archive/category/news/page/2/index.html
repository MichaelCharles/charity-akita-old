2014-2015 Akita Association of JETs Financial Report

The following is a list of the events hosted by AAJ in the 2014-2015 year, the individual event proceeds (in yen) and the charities they were donated to. The financial year for AAJ begins with the Welcome Party, and ends with the Farewell Party.

Welcome Party 146,611 Misonotenshien Orphanage
Lake Tazawa Camping 32,000 Misonotenshien Orphanage
Halloween Party 464,000 Peace Winds Japan
Soup Curry Quiz 22,500 Japan Cat Network
 Caroling 57,654 Toys & Books for Akita Orphanages
Appi Ski Trip N/A N/A
Valentine’s Masquerade 5,000 Japan Red Cross
St. Patrick’s Party 73,000 Japan Red Cross
Hanami Tea Party N/A N/A
International Sumo Tournament 80,156 Save the Children Japan
Farewell Party 10,000 Equal Marriage Alliance Japan

*A small amount of proceeds were also raised/donated via the AAJ Book Exchange for the Japan Red Cross

The total value of proceeds donated during the 2014-2015 JET year was 914,310円.

At this time, all the proceeds raised in the 2014-2015 year have been donated to the charities specified above, and all money owed to AAJ has been paid in full. Below is the exact value donated to each charity as verified by the donation receipts on record from last year.

  • Akita Misonotenshien Orphanage: 200,000円
  • Akita Misonotenshien Orphanage (Toys & Books): 57,654円
  • Peace Winds Japan: 464,000円
  • Japan Cat Network: 22,500円
  • Japan Red Cross: 80,000円
  • Save the Children: 80,156円
  • Equal Marriage Alliance (EMA) Japan: 10,000円

A note about the 2014-2015 year:

Unfortunately, the records from last year are not exactly clear about the total amount of proceeds made at each event (the documents we have show some conflicting information), but the numbers do match up fairly well with the values shown on the donation receipts. The numbers in the table above are believed to be generally correct; however, one notable exception is that 200,000円 was donated to the Misonotenshien Orphanage (confirmed by the receipt), but our records show only 178,611円 was raised from the Welcome Party & Tazawako Camping. We don’t have a clear record to confirm exactly where the extra donation money came from, but fortunately, from this point onward, our new record keeping procedures that will prevent this confusion from arising again.

For any further information about past AAJ events and proceeds, please check the Akita JET Wiki,  http://akitajet.com/wiki/AAJ#Annual_Events

Thank you all for your generosity and support in the 2014-2015 year!

Transparency

One of the main issues brought up consistently by the community in the past month or so has been transparency. People want to know, rightly, what is being done and by whom.

So in the interest of transparency, I want to answer a few questions that I expect some people might have concerning this website, who is running it, and what the website’s future might be.

Who are you?

I am Michael Aubrey, a second year prefectural ALT living in Akita City. I am not an AAJ team member.

If you’re not an AAJ team member, why did you create this website?

Initially because I felt like the AAJ Elections Poll email didn’t present the information in a clean and clear way. I took it upon myself to throw together the site archived here as an alternative and I presented it to the AAJ team to use.

I created the current incarnation of this website to offer to the AAJ team as an alternative to using SurveyMonkey. While it was clear in the recent poll that elections had won, the system was less than desirable. The poll received a total of 50 votes, but only 39 of them were confirmed legitimate through email or Facebook postings. While in this case, elections was clearly the winner even if you removed the unclaimed votes, the actual elections themselves might be much closer.

What’s going on under the hood?

I’m using WordPress as a CMS. I wanted the new WordPress powered version of the site to look similar to the old “Elections Poll” site. I started with the Flatly theme by Mark Simmons, and then I edited it until I was (mostly) satisfied with the overall look. I am not a professional web developer. I gutted code from other themes, wrote plenty of my own using outdated standards and looking things up along the way, and deleted huge chunks of unwanted parts of the theme. Under the hood, this site really is a messy, ugly, Frankenstein’s monster: But I think the frontend looks alright.

The main reason for using WordPress was so that I could use its vast library of plugins to create a setup where users could sign up through a non-automated system and then participate in an anonymous survey that would allow only one vote per user.

New user registrations are being handled simply by a form created by the “Ninja Forms” plugin. It simply collects the data and sends an email to admin@charityakita.com saying, “Hey, make an account for this person.” WordPress does have a built in automated registration process, but I’ve disabled it in order to try and prevent people from creating multiple accounts.

For the election page, I decided to use the “WP Survey and Quiz Tool” plugin. It meets our needs in that it keeps users from voting more than once based on their username, while at the same time only recording whether or not each user had voted, and not what they voted, keeping things anonymous. However, the plugin doesn’t have a built in way to keep visitors who are not logged in from participating. To resolve this I built a WordPress template to be used on survey pages which forces the user the sign in.

I’m sure it isn’t a perfect solution, but it is a far cry better than relying on SurveyMonkey with people writing in whether or not they had voted to verify votes.

So how will voting work now?

First you’ll need a username. This process is not automated, so you should sign up as soon as possible. The New User Application Form is found here. I have to manually add each user, so it might take some time for you to get your account. I’m going to try to add users at least once every 24 hours leading up to the election. The poll will close at 10pm on July 27.

On Thursday July 23, a new “News” post will be made linking you to candidate profiles and giving you a link to the election poll. Then you simply sign in and fill out the form.

This information will be repeated in future emails and in the coming news posts as well, but I want to repeat it as often as possible so that people don’t attempt to sign up at 9:30pm on July 27 and then complain that they didn’t get their account on time.

Why should we trust you?

The lack of an objective third party to conduct these elections is something we can’t really avoid. It is one of the many reasons why I’m not comfortable with them in the first place but that’s a whole other conversation that is done and over. PAs, block leaders, and current AAJ members can all run, meaning that unless we employed some organization outside our community and completely unaffiliated with us, we’re going to run into some kind of trust issues.

I’m open to suggestions to making this whole ordeal more trustworthy. I made the website because I saw a need and tried to fill it as best as I could, but I understand given the current climate that some might question my motivates.

Who will run this site in the future?

Hopefully, if the future AAJ wants to use this website, they will. WordPress takes very little technical expertise to run once it has been set up. If you can use Facebook, you can use WordPress well enough to add new pages or posts. In addition to having access to the Admin account, current active AAJ team members (Alana and Dave) have “Editor” level accounts, meaning they can contribute content as easily as me.

When the new AAJ team is chosen, the password on the Admin account will be changed and given to that team. In addition, their Charity Akita user accounts will be elevated to “Editor” level.

I have more questions!

Cool. Feel free to post any questions, concerns, or suggestions you have either on the discussion in the Akita JET Facebook group, or in the comments section below.
